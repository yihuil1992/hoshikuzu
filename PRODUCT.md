# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hoshikuzu is for a small audience of makers, players, and curious visitors who arrive through a quiet personal index of tools and experiments. They are not here to be sold to. They are scanning for signals: a useful calculator, a timer, a work sample, a small crafted interaction, or a link worth opening.

The primary context is exploratory and low-pressure. A visitor may spend only a few seconds in the starfield, but the interface should reward lingering without demanding attention.

## Product Purpose

Hoshikuzu gathers small tools, production work, and drifting experiments into one coherent night-sky interface. The home experience acts as a stardust index: each star is a mapped object, and each object points to a usable screen or artifact.

Success means the site feels calm, precise, and memorable while remaining practical. The interaction should make discovery feel intentional, not decorative. The utility pages should feel like they belong to the same observatory as the home starfield.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, archival, celestial.

The product should feel like a private star atlas with working instruments attached: restrained, attentive, slightly mysterious, and precise. It should carry the intimacy of a personal notebook without becoming casual or messy.

## Anti-references

- Do not make it feel like a generic SaaS landing page with oversized hero metrics, repeated feature cards, and conversion copy.
- Do not make it feel like a neon sci-fi HUD, cyberpunk control panel, or game menu.
- Do not use heavy glassmorphism, bokeh blobs, purple-blue gradient wash, or decorative glow as the default answer.
- Do not use real nebula imagery as a fixed screen background. It reads as a foreground sticker when the 3D scene rotates.
- Do not let the 2D UI compete with the starfield. The interface should annotate the sky, not cover it.

## Design Principles

1. **Annotate, do not decorate.** 2D UI should behave like labels, index marks, and observatory notes layered over a sky.
2. **Silence is structure.** Empty space, alignment, and subtle hierarchy should carry more weight than ornamental effects.
3. **Make motion feel inevitable.** Camera movement and UI transitions should be single-stage, smooth, and physically plausible.
4. **Keep tools usable.** Even poetic surfaces must support clear scanning, obvious actions, and readable information.
5. **Let stars own the light.** Accents and glow should come from interactive objects and state changes, not from page-wide decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to readable contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, clear focus states, and reduced-motion-safe interaction choices. Motion should remain calm and short, with no flashing, rapid pulsing, or forced parallax. Text should stay legible over dark backgrounds, especially for labels, tooltips, form controls, and footer links.
